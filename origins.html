Descendents of Noah

Now these are the records of the generations of Shem, Ham, and JAPHETH, the sons of Noah; and sons were born to them after the flood. Genesis 10:1

To the Greeks, Japheth was Iapetus, the Titan god of mortaliy, the father of Prometheus, and thus the ancestral father to all Indo-Europeans.

Descendents of Japheth

The sons of Japheth were Gomer and Magog and Madai and JAVAN and Tubal and Meshech and Tiras...

The sons of Javan were ELISHAH and Tarshish, Kittim and Dodanim.

From these the coastlands of the nations were separated into their lands, every one according to his language, according to their families, into their nations. Genesis 10:2,4–5

Most scholars equate Javan to the Greek god Ion, son of Apollo,
and forefather of one of the four ancient Greek tribes: the Ionians.
In the Bible, 'Javan' also refers to Greece, as in the Book of Daniel,
which references the king of the Javans as Alexander (the Great).
Elishah is also known as Eliseus, not to be confused with the prophet Elisha.
The mythical Elysium, Elysian Fields, and the ancient Greek Aeolians,
all originate in reference to Eliseus/Elishah and his descendents.

Book of Jasher

And the children of Elishah are the ALMANIM, and they also went and built themselves cities; those are the cities situate between the mountains of Job and Shibathmo; and of them were the people of LUMBARDI who dwell opposite the mountains of Job and Shibathmo, and they conquered the land of Italia and remained there unto this day. Sefer haYashar 10:15

The Book of Jasher mentioned in the Hebrew Bible has unfortunately been lost over the centuries.
Multiple forgeries have been published, but only the Sefer haYashar midrash expands upon the original references found in Joshua and 2 Samuel.

All the Men


The Almanim were a confederation of Germanic Tribes, which the Romans called the Alemanni in 213 CE. They later dwelled along the Upper Rhine and Black Forest, remained independent until becoming a duchy under the Franks, then the duchy of Swabia under the Holy Roman Empire. This confederation of tribes is the root origin of the ALLMON family name.
