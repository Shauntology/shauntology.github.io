Remembering Torao Yamamizu

Dec 9, 1914 - Jan 28, 1942

Torao Yamamizu was born in Pepeekeo, Hawaii in Dec 1914 to Ichiji and Haru (Tomota) Yamamizu.

He attended Waiakeakai Elementary and Internediate Schools, as well as Hilo Intermediate School.

He worked as a fisherman for Marine Products Co., Ltd, in Hilo before being inducted into the Army in November 1941 at age 26.

On Jan 28, 1942, after completing trainbing at Schofield Boot Camp the day before, he was killed in action on the way back to Hilo aboard the Royal T Frank. He had no chance to survive the Japanese torpedo that hit where he was sleeping below deck, as their ship sank in under 1 minute.

Families were not notified at the the time, and survivors were threatened under martial law to never speak of the incident. It took 58 years until these men were finally honored for their service.
